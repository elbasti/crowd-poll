Welcome to Wiki-Polls

Wiki-polls is a open-source, open-analytics platform for crowdsourced polling data. Our goal is to provide a tool for building centralized polling data that is open to the public, providing free statistical plug-ins with supporting materials.

About Wiki-Polls

Crowdsourced Polling

Typical poll aggregation is done by journalists and academics and stored in private databases. Wiki-polls removes the veil that surrounds polling analytics by leveraging the power of an informed, engaged, online community.

Open-Source Analytics

Using the crowdsourced polling database, wiki-polls constructs a set of useful analytics, digestible graphs, and easy to understand synthesis of the data for each poll. Each piece is customizable, and open-source.

About Wiki-polls.
It'll blow your mind.

There are hundreds of national election polls taken leading up to a presidential election. If we consider state-level polls, this number can creep up into the thousands. Instead of letting news outlets 'synthesize' the data, why shouldn't we have a single, publicly accessible repository of all polls related to a particular race? Wiki-polls fills this void, providing a one-stop internet address for all data political.

Crowdsourced Polling. Anything you can do, we can do better.

News outlets hire individuals to aggregate the polling data of major market research companies, synthesizing the raw numbers into digestible snippets. Rarely is the individual able to access these disparate sources of information themselves. We can't all spend our time aggregating data! Crowdsourced data generation is a solution to this problem. It allows active, engaged participation by relying on multiple people for minimal work. The result is a database which competes with privately sourced competition in scope and detail, while being open to all who care to delve into the data.

Open Analytics
Statistics and Math done out in the open for once.

Even when the data is available, the results of 'analytics' are published with minimal detail of how and why certain quantitative choices were made. Wiki-polls provides a basic framework for analyzing polling data, giving basic plots and statistical analysis of likely outcomes. We also crowdsource the analytics, allowing users to submit their own work (with proper documentation). The other users are then able to vote on the various analyses, ask questions and give constructive feedback. Wiki-polls then synthesizes these votes into a crowdsourced analytics page, presenting the most talked about analyses and reports.
